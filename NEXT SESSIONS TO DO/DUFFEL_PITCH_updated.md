## The Opportunity in One Paragraph

England fans attending the 2026 FIFA World Cup face a logistical challenge no existing travel app solves completely: group stage matches are spread across multiple US cities, requiring 2–3 inter-city flights, car hire at each destination, and 3–5 days of unplanned time between games. Refueler is a Bitcoin-native rewards platform built on real-time mobility data. We are integrating Duffel's flights and car hire API to give our users a seamless, sats-rewarded World Cup travel experience — and we'd like to discuss a formal partnership before the tournament begins.

---

## What Refueler Is

Refueler is a predictive commerce platform that uses real-time transport data (National Rail Darwin, TfL, geofencing) to connect mobile users with food and drink vendors at the precise moment they need them. A commuter's train departs Limehouse; four minutes later their coffee is ready at London Fenchurch Street. An EV driver plugs in at Lakeside; their M&S flat white is at their bay in 4:22.

Users earn Bitcoin (sats) on every purchase made through the platform. Not points. Not vouchers. Actual Bitcoin, issued via Lightning Network, accumulated in their Minibits wallet.

The platform is live and operational. The Command Centre dashboard runs in production. The Supabase backend is live. The Mapbox geofencing is active.

## The World Cup Use Case

**The problem:** An England fan with tickets to Group B games in Miami, New York, and Atlanta needs:
- 3 inter-city flights
- Car hire at each destination
- Somewhere to stay near each stadium
- Something to do for 3–5 days between each game
- Food and drink on match day near the venue

No single app handles all of this. Existing travel apps (Skyscanner, Kayak, Booking.com) handle parts of it with no intelligence about the match schedule, no coordination between legs, and zero Bitcoin reward.

**The Refueler solution with Duffel:**

```
Fan opens Refueler → England skin active (St George's Cross theme)
Match schedule loaded from FIFA fixture data
        ↓
"Book your World Cup travel" card appears
Duffel API: flight search Miami → New York → Atlanta → London
Duffel API: car hire at each destination airport
        ↓
Fan books all three legs in one flow
Refueler earns developer margin
Fan earns sats on each booking (~17,000 sats per £400 flight at 1% rebate)
        ↓
Match day: stadium geofence active, pre-order food near venue
Inter-match days: Roadtrippers curated excursion itinerary
        ↓
All purchases earn sats. Trip becomes a Bitcoin accumulation event.
```

## Why This Works for Duffel

**New distribution channel:** Refueler's user base is Bitcoin-native, mobile-first, and motivated to use the app for financial reasons (sats accumulation). These are high-intent travel bookers — they have tickets already, they need flights, and they'll book through whichever app makes it frictionless.

**Tournament timing:** World Cup 2026 is a one-time commercial event with a defined, high-value audience. England fans travelling to three group stage games in the US represent significant flight and car hire spend per user. The average England fan's World Cup trip is estimated at £3,000–£6,000 total. Flight and car hire alone: £1,500–£3,000.

**Sats as a differentiator:** No other Duffel distribution partner is offering Bitcoin rewards on bookings. "Book your World Cup flights and earn Bitcoin" is a headline that travels in Bitcoin-native communities (Twitter/X, Nostr, podcasts) without paid marketing spend.

**Alignment with Duffel's model:** Refueler pays Duffel's standard developer fees per booking. We are not asking for custom pricing or exclusivity. We are a new distribution channel that brings qualified, high-intent users to Duffel's API.
