01 Data moat
Now
2
→
12 mo
4

The core proprietary dataset is the correlation between Darwin STOMP train departure events and actual order fulfilment times — the 4:22 metric is the first data point. Over time, Refueler accumulates a unique ETA accuracy model: which stations produce which lead times, how platform layout, queue position, and time-of-day interact. No competitor can buy this dataset — it is generated only by live orders flowing through the system. Secondary datasets: geofence dwell patterns, NFC/QR handover hit rates, vendor throughput capacity by hour, sats reward elasticity (how reward rate affects order frequency).

At 12 months: operator-level C2C throughput model; per-vendor capacity heatmaps; demand surge predictions around football fixture calendar. The dataset becomes a feedback loop — better predictions → higher order success rate → more merchant retention → more data.

Weakness: The dataset is essentially empty until live orders begin. The moat does not exist yet — it must be earned through deployment. Pre-launch, a better-funded competitor with rail API access and existing merchant relationships could enter first. Speed of merchant onboarding is the critical path to this moat.
02 Network effects
Now
1
→
12 mo

Three network effect vectors exist, each at different maturity stages:

Vendor-side (supply): Each new merchant added to a station catchment improves the platform's utility for all users on that line. A commuter who previously had one pre-order option (M&S) gains optionality as Black Sheep, cluster C2C vendors, and eventually stadium-proximate restaurants join. More vendors → longer session dwell → more order attempts → more sats earned. This is currently a single-vendor moat at Fenchurch Street and is therefore fragile.

User-side (demand): A denser user base on any given train generates aggregate foot flow signals useful for vendor staffing predictions. This is currently theoretical — no demand-side network effect exists until multiple users are active on the same route simultaneously. At 50+ daily active commuters on C2C, the signal becomes meaningful.

Cross-side (Bitcoin wallet): The Minibits/Lightning wallet layer creates a proto-network effect in the Bitcoin community — users who earn sats at Refueler vendors become advocates in Bitcoin-native channels (Nostr, Telegram, Fountain), driving further user acquisition with zero paid CAC. This is the strongest current network signal but is soft (word-of-mouth) rather than structural.

Weakness: Classic chicken-and-egg. Network effects only activate with density. The platform has zero structural network effects prior to launch. This is the weakest current moat score and is unlikely to meaningfully improve until Month 3–4 of live operations.
03 Switching costs
Now

Switching costs operate differently on each side of the marketplace:

Vendor switching cost: The partner tablet app integrates into operational workflow — staff learn the queue interface, the countdown display, and the PLACED → MAKING → READY → COLLECTED state machine. This is low-friction to adopt but moderately costly to unlearn. The foot flow data dependency (Opening Hours Advisory, Match Day Mode, advance demand signals) becomes operationally valuable within weeks of deployment. A vendor that switches loses forward-planning intelligence they cannot easily replicate from another source. At 12 months: session-tagged revenue reporting and the CFO-ready financial dashboard further embed Refueler into the merchant's back-office.

User switching cost: The primary mechanism is sats accumulation. Bitcoin earned via Lightning/Minibits is genuinely the user's money — not a proprietary points balance. This reduces lock-in relative to a closed loyalty scheme. However, the order history, saved preferences, and habitual usage pattern constitute behavioural lock-in. The daily commuter who pre-orders every morning builds a routine that competing platforms must break. Inertia is an underrated switching cost.

Weakness: Sats are real money and therefore portable — a user can take their accumulated value to any Lightning-compatible wallet or merchant. This is philosophically correct (Bitcoin ethos) but structurally limits the financial lock-in a traditional loyalty programme would provide. The behavioural and convenience lock-in must compensate.

The privacy architecture is a structural differentiator, not a compliance checkbox. The geofence entry event is processed entirely on-device — no location data transmitted, no server-side inference, no third-party data sale. This is auditable and verifiable by any GDPR regulator or technical reviewer. The approved positioning — "Your phone works it out locally. We never see where you are" — is a factual claim that ad-tech competitors structurally cannot make.

GDPR moat mechanism: As UK/EU enforcement tightens around location data (ICO investigations, DSA enforcement), platforms that monetise location surveillance face increasing compliance cost and reputational risk. Refueler's architecture becomes comparatively cheaper to operate and easier to defend. This is a cost-of-compliance moat that grows as regulation intensifies.

Duffel managed content layer: IATA-accredited managed content access requires Duffel's contractual relationship with airlines. New entrants cannot replicate this independently — they must either build their own IATA accreditation (2–3 year process, significant capital) or use an aggregator like Duffel. Refueler's early partnership conversation creates a first-mover positioning with a named partner before the World Cup window closes.

Weakness: The GDPR position requires the dedicated compliance session (Website Build Phase) to be formally documented and audited. Until the privacy policy, GDPR disclosure, and on-device architecture statement are published, the moat exists technically but is not yet investor or regulator-legible. Draft and publish this session before investor conversations.

06 Timing moat — why now
Now
4
→
12 mo

Four infrastructure curves have converged simultaneously, making this product buildable now when it was not possible two years ago:

Lightning Network
Sub-second settlement, near-zero fees, Minibits ecash layer for offline UX. The payment rail is production-ready. 2022 this was experimental; today it is deployable.
Darwin open data
Network Rail's STOMP feed provides real-time train movement data at no cost. The predictive ordering trigger is built on free public infrastructure — not a negotiated API contract.
EV charging density
Lakeside, O2, and retail park EV bays have reached the dwell-time threshold (20–40 min) that makes predictive ordering commercially viable. The Supercharger network is the distribution layer.
Minibits ecash
Cashu protocol / Minibits brings bearer-instrument privacy to small-value sats rewards. The UX for non-technical users is now viable — no node management, no channel liquidity required.

The timing score stays at 4 at 12 months because this window is not permanent. As EV charging becomes ubiquitous and Lightning becomes mainstream, the early-mover advantage of the 4:22 benchmark and the established merchant relationships will be the residual asset. The timing moat converts into a data and relationship moat.

Investor narrative — moat summary
Refueler's defensibility compounds across three interlocking layers. The first is architectural: on-device geofencing and Lightning-native payments create a privacy posture and community trust that well-funded competitors are structurally excluded from replicating — you cannot buy your way into the Bitcoin-native distribution channel, and you cannot retrofit privacy-first architecture onto a surveillance-based loyalty platform. The second is temporal: the data moat — the ETA accuracy model, per-vendor capacity heatmaps, and foot flow intelligence — does not yet exist, but it accretes with every live order, and a late entrant cannot compress that timeline. The third is relational: the 4:22 benchmark was earned in the field and anchors a merchant trust dynamic that a clone cannot replicate with code. The honest assessment is that today the moat is narrow and the most dangerous competitive scenario is a Bitcoin-native copycat moving before Refueler locks its anchor merchant agreements in writing. The immediate priority is formalising the M&S partnership and onboarding the C2C cluster — those signed relationships, more than any technical infrastructure, are what an investor is ultimately buying.
