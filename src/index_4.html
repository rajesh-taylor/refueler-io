Architecture principle
Useful software doesn't require surveilling the people who use it. Your device detects when your train is moving and fires the order trigger locally. What reaches our server is an order — not a location event. Sessions use short-lived tokens that expire when you close the tab. No tracking scripts. No third-party calls. No cookie banners, because there is nothing to consent to.
📱
On-device geofencing only

Geofence logic runs entirely in your phone's OS location framework. Entry events are never transmitted. We couldn't build a location profile of you even if we wanted to.

🔑
Ephemeral sessions

Authentication uses short-lived tokens via magic link — no persistent cookies, no long-lived tracking identifiers. Close the tab, the session ends.

⚡
Your wallet, your keys

Your sats balance lives in your Minibits wallet — not our database. We record that a reward was issued. We don't hold your funds, and we can't freeze them.

🚫
Zero third-party scripts

No analytics. No pixels. No A/B tools. This page loads from Cloudflare's edge — nothing else is called. Verify with your browser's network inspector right now.

03 / LOCATION

The location architecture — in full

The Passive Ambient Awareness feature operates entirely on your device using your phone's OS geofencing APIs (iOS Core Location / Android Geofence API). Here is the complete data flow:

Step 1: Geofence configured on-device
When you opt in, your phone's OS registers a geofence boundary around trigger stations. This registration happens entirely within your phone's location framework — no coordinates are sent to our servers.
Step 2: Entry detected on-device
When your OS detects geofence entry, it fires a local notification: "Your flat white will be ready in 4 mins — confirm?" The geofence entry event itself is never transmitted.
Step 3: You confirm the order
If you tap Confirm, your phone sends an order request containing: item, vendor ID, and session token. It does not contain your location, the geofence that triggered it, or any GPS coordinates.
Step 4: No location data in our database
Our database has no location column in the orders table. There is nowhere for location data to be stored. This is an architectural guarantee, not only a policy promise.
"Refueler knows your train is moving. Your phone works it out locally. We never see where you are."

This feature requires explicit opt-in at onboarding. You can disable it at any time in Settings → Location → Off without affecting your ability to order or earn sats.

07 / COOKIES

Cookies — the honest answer

We do not use cookies for tracking, analytics, advertising, or profiling. The only cookie that may be set is:

Supabase session token (strictly necessary)
A short-lived JWT set after magic link authentication. It contains your anonymous user ID and an expiry timestamp. It expires when you close the tab or within 1 hour — whichever comes first. No persistent login cookies are set.

There are no advertising cookies, analytics cookies, or third-party cookies on refueler.io. This is why you see no cookie banner. There is nothing to consent to beyond the strictly necessary session token, which is covered by your agreement to the terms of service.

Verify this yourself: open browser developer tools → Application → Cookies. You will see at most one Supabase auth token and nothing else.

08 / BITCOIN & SATS

What we know about your wallet

Sats rewards are issued to your Minibits ecash wallet via the Lightning Network.

We record
The amount of sats awarded per transaction, the timestamp, and the vendor — a financial record required for our own accounting.
We do not record
Your Lightning address, Minibits wallet identifier, node pubkey, or any on-chain Bitcoin address after the payment is sent.
Ecash privacy
Minibits uses the Cashu protocol, which provides bearer-instrument privacy. Your sats balance is held as an ecash token on your device — not in our database. We cannot see your balance, freeze your funds, or prevent you from spending your rewards.

You own the asset from the moment it is issued. Not after some future redemption event that we control.
